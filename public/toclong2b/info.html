Non-Passable for All Types of Vehicles (NPATV) - Red Alert

This means that the road or route is not accessible to any type of vehicle, including heavy-duty vehicles, emergency vehicles, and off-road vehicles. This level is usually issued during extreme weather conditions, such as severe flooding, landslides where the road is completely blocked due to high water depth.

When the depth of the flood reaches the measurement of at least 20cm above (approximately measured as the tire level of a light vehicle), the alert level is declared as NPATV.


Non-Passable for Light Vehicles (NPLV) - Orange Alert

This means that the road or route is not accessible to light vehicles, such as cars, motorcycles, and bicycles. However, heavy-duty vehicles, emergency vehicles, and off-road vehicles may still be able to pass through. This level is typically issued when the road is partially blocked due to a certain water depth, making it unsafe for small vehicles to travel.

When the depth of the flood reaches the measurement of at least 10cm above (approximately measured as half of the tire level of a light vehicle) up to 20cm (approximately measured as knee level of a person in average height), the alert level is declared as NPLV.


Passable for All Types of Vehicles (PATV) - Clear

This means that the road or route is accessible to all types of vehicles, including light vehicles, heavy-duty vehicles, emergency vehicles, and off-road vehicles. This level is typically issued when the road is clear and safe for travel. However, drivers are advised to exercise caution and adhere to any speed limits or traffic rules in place.

If depth of the flood is at the measurement of 10cm or below (approximately measured as half of the knee level of a person's average height) the alert level is declared as PATV.


Hazard: Flooding



What is Flood?

Flooding is the overflow of water from a river or another body of water due to heavy rainfall.


What To Do During Flood

  • Be prepared to evacuate immediately when there’s an alert for heavy rainfall.
  • Refrain from walking through floodwater most especially without wearing protective gear like boots.
  • Don’t walk or drive through moving water. It can sweep you and your car away.
  • Turn off all electrical appliances and LPG tank; turn off the main power switch as needed.

Survival Kit

  • Water: one gallon per person
  • Food: non-perishable, easy-to-prepare items
  • Flashlight
  • First-Aid Kit
  • Medications and medical items
  • Sanitation and personal hygiene
  • Copies of personal documents (IDs, proof of address, passports, insurance policies, birth certificates)
  • Cellphone with chargers
  • Family and emergency contact information
  • Extra cash
  • Emergency blanket

Hazard: Storm Surge



What is a Storm Surge?

A storm surge is a tsunami-like phenomenon of rising water resulting from typhoons (storms).



How To Prepare For Storm Surges

  • Evacuate immediately and move to a more elevated area when there’s an alert for strong typhoons.
  • Always have a survival kit ready and accessible. Ideally this is near your exit doors, ready for grabbing when in a rush.
  • Listen for emergency information and alerts
